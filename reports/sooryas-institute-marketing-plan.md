# Marketing Plan: Soorya's Institute

Prepared for: Soorya's Skin Hair and Makeup, Kumarapuram, Thiruvananthapuram  
Business name: Soorya's Institute  
Date: 4 June 2026  
Purpose: First-batch admissions plan for a 4-month professional beauty-care course

## 1. Marketing Objective

The first marketing objective is not brand awareness in a broad sense. The objective is to fill the first professional batch with the right students.

Primary target:

| Metric | Target |
|---|---:|
| Batch size | 15 students |
| Course fee | INR 60,000 |
| Booking amount | INR 10,000 |
| Minimum go/no-go paid bookings | 12 students |
| Qualified enquiries required | 75-100 |
| Counselling calls or visits | 35-45 |
| Demo-day attendees | 20-30 |
| Campaign period | 45-60 days |

The strongest proof of market demand is not likes, shares, or verbal interest. It is paid booking amounts.

## 2. Core Positioning

Recommended positioning statement:

> Soorya's Institute is a founder-led, salon-embedded beauty-care finishing school at Kumarapuram, helping women move from classroom learning to real salon confidence through practical training inside a working ladies' beauty parlour.

This positioning should be repeated across brochure, WhatsApp, posters, reels, and counselling scripts.

## 3. Target Segments

### 3.1 Job Seekers

Likely profile:

- young women after 10th, +2, degree, diploma, or job break;
- women looking for salon employment;
- students who want practical skills more than theory-heavy courses.

Message:

> Learn practical beauty-care skills in Malayalam and prepare for salon/parlour work with confidence.

Primary proof points:

- live parlour exposure;
- practical group training;
- portfolio photos;
- certificate of completion;
- interview and client-handling guidance.

### 3.2 Freelance and Home-Based Beauticians

Likely profile:

- homemakers;
- women who want income from home;
- women who can serve neighbours, friends, wedding functions, and local clients.

Message:

> Start earning from home or on-call beauty services with a clear service menu, pricing guidance, and practical training.

Primary proof points:

- starter service menu;
- pricing templates;
- WhatsApp and Instagram promotion guidance;
- client consultation practice;
- hygiene and product-use discipline.

### 3.3 Future Small-Parlour Owners

Likely profile:

- women who want to start a small ladies' parlour;
- existing informal service providers;
- families considering a small self-employment investment.

Message:

> Learn beauty-care skills plus the basics of running a small ladies' parlour.

Primary proof points:

- salon operations exposure;
- appointment handling;
- consumables costing;
- customer follow-up;
- business basics.

## 4. Offer Design

Course name:

**4-Month Professional Beauty Care Career Program**

Fee:

INR 60,000

Suggested payment plan:

| Stage | Amount |
|---|---:|
| Admission booking | INR 10,000 |
| Month 1 | INR 15,000 |
| Month 2 | INR 15,000 |
| Month 3 | INR 10,000 |
| Month 4 | INR 10,000 |
| Total | INR 60,000 |

Batch design:

- 15 total students;
- practical sessions split into Group A and Group B;
- 7-8 students per practical group;
- Malayalam-supported instruction for comfort and clarity;
- live-parlour environment;
- final practical evaluation and certificate.

Offer guardrails:

- clearly state what is included in the fee;
- clearly state whether personal kits are included or separately purchased;
- publish attendance requirements;
- publish refund policy;
- avoid any government-recognition claim unless formally obtained.

## 5. Message Architecture

### 5.1 Main Campaign Line

**Train in a Real Parlour. Build a Real Beauty-Care Career.**

### 5.2 Supporting Lines

- For job seekers: **Prepare for salon and beauty-parlour work with practical training.**
- For freelancers: **Learn services you can offer from home or on-call.**
- For future owners: **Understand skills, pricing, hygiene, and small-parlour basics.**
- For families: **A practical skill course with visible career and income pathways.**

### 5.3 Trust Builders

- Soorya is the proprietor and trainer.

## 6. Marketing Funnel

The funnel should be simple and WhatsApp-led.

| Stage | Goal | Tool |
|---|---|---|
| Awareness | Make people know the course exists | Posters, reels, client referrals, local groups |
| Interest | Get them to ask for details | WhatsApp, Instagram DM, phone |
| Trust | Explain value and answer objections | Malayalam brochure, counselling call, demo day |
| Commitment | Collect booking amount | Payment link/UPI, receipt, app entry |
| Retention | Keep student engaged before start | WhatsApp updates, orientation, kit info |

Every campaign item should lead to one action:

> WhatsApp for syllabus, fee, and admission details.

## 7. Channel Plan

### 7.1 WhatsApp

Role: main admissions channel.

Actions:

- create a dedicated admissions WhatsApp number or label;
- use quick replies for fee, syllabus, location, batch date, and payment plan;
- send Malayalam brochure PDF;
- maintain enquiry status in the app;
- follow up after 1 day, 3 days, and 7 days.

WhatsApp labels:

- New enquiry;
- Brochure sent;
- Counselling done;
- Demo day invited;
- Booking pending;
- Paid booking;
- Not interested;
- Future batch.

### 7.2 Instagram and Facebook

Role: trust and discovery.

Content types:

- trainer introduction;
- parlour walk-through;
- tool/product setup;
- demo clips;
- common beauty-care mistakes;
- student outcome explanation;
- Malayalam Q&A reels;
- fee/payment plan explanation;

- seat availability posts.

Posting rhythm:

- 4 reels per week;
- 3 story updates per week;
- 1 student/career post per week;

Role: high-trust referral source.

Actions:

- place a small course standee at reception;
- give each client a Malayalam course card;
- ask satisfied customers for referrals;
- offer referral reward as a service coupon or product discount.

Suggested referral offer:

> Refer a student who joins the course and receive a beauty-service voucher worth INR 1,000.

- Soorya introduction;

Demo-day conversion target:

|---|---:|

| Same-day bookings | 5-8 |

## 10. Admissions Counselling Script

Counselling should discover the student's goal before explaining the course.

Opening:

> Are you planning to learn beauty care for a job, home/freelance income, or to start your own parlour later?

Key questions:

- Have you learned any beauty-care work before?
- Are you comfortable attending practical classes regularly for 4 months?
- Which area are you coming from?
- Do you want to work in a salon, freelance, or start a small business?
- Would you like to attend the demo session before booking?

Closing:

> The batch has only 15 seats, and practical sessions will be split into smaller groups. Admission is confirmed with a INR 10,000 booking amount. Shall I send the payment and admission details?

## 11. Objection Handling

| Objection | Response Direction |
|---|---|
| Fee is high | Explain 4-month duration, live-parlour training, smaller practical groups, career/freelance/business modules, and payment plan |
| Need government certificate | Clarify current certificate honestly; explain curriculum alignment intent; do not misrepresent recognition |
| I am not confident | Emphasize beginner-friendly practical sessions, smaller groups, and gradual movement from demo to supervised service |
| Family needs to decide | Send brochure and invite family to demo day |
| Distance is an issue | Share exact location, timing options, and group schedule |
| Can I earn after course? | Explain realistic pathways: salon job, assisted freelance, home services, portfolio building; avoid guaranteed-income claims |

## 15. Strategic Recommendation

Soorya's Institute should market the first batch as a premium but accessible career program, not as a casual beauty class.

The campaign should lead with:

- real-parlour apprenticeship;
- founder-led training by Soorya;
- practical groups of 7-8 students;
- job, freelance, and small-business outcomes;
- Soorya as the expert proprietor-trainer;
- limited first batch of 15 students.

The first campaign should run for 45-60 days. If 12 students do not pay the booking amount, the institute should extend admissions or revise the offer before spending heavily on the full setup.
