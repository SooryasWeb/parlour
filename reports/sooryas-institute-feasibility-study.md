# Feasibility Study: Soorya's Institute

Prepared for: Soorya's Skin Hair and Makeup, Kumarapuram, Thiruvananthapuram  
Business name: Soorya's Institute  
Date: 4 June 2026  
Prepared as: Market feasibility and business coaching note

## 1. Executive Summary

Soorya's Institute is feasible as a boutique, founder-led beauty-care apprenticeship institute operated from the existing premises of Soorya's Skin Hair and Makeup at Kumarapuram, Thiruvananthapuram.

The strongest model is not a low-fee, high-volume academy. The recommended model is a practical, salon-attached professional course for women who want one of three outcomes:

- employment in salons and beauty parlours;
- home-based or freelance beauty-care income;
- preparation to start a small ladies' beauty parlour.

The proposed first batch model is financially attractive:

| Parameter | Proposed First Batch |
|---|---:|
| Batch size | 15 students |
| Course duration | 4 months |
| Fee per student | INR 60,000 |
| Total batch revenue | INR 9,00,000 |
| Monthly revenue equivalent | INR 2,25,000 |
| Training format | Founder-led, practical-heavy, live-parlour apprenticeship with split practical groups |
| Trainer | Soorya, proprietor and expert tutor |
| Trainer salary allocation | INR 60,000/month |
| Premises rent paid by institute | INR 10,000/month |
| External trainer rental income | INR 8,000/month assumed |

The earlier physical capacity input was 5-10 students at one time. Therefore, the recommended operating method is one administrative batch of 15 students, but with practical sessions split into two groups of 7-8 students. This keeps the economics strong while protecting training quality.

Final feasibility conclusion: proceed with the first batch, provided admissions are validated before major incremental spending, practical sessions are split, and marketing is launched at least 45-60 days before the batch start date.

## 2. Business Concept

Soorya's Institute will provide beauty-care training in Malayalam from an existing ladies' beauty parlour in Kumarapuram. The premise already has a working beauty-care identity, reducing the risk normally faced by new training institutes.

Recommended positioning:

> A founder-led beauty-care apprenticeship inside a working ladies' parlour, helping women become job-ready, freelance-ready, or small-parlour-ready through real salon discipline, practical confidence, and business-ready habits.

This positioning is stronger than "beautician course" because it connects training to income pathways and uses the parlour itself as the training advantage.

## 2.1 Recommended USP

Recommended USP:

Short version for ads and posters:

Website headline option:

Admissions pitch:

Malayalam instruction should be retained as an accessibility feature, but it should not be presented as the main moat. The actual moat is the combination of:

- a real operating ladies' parlour;
- founder-led training by Soorya;
- small practical groups;
- client-handling and hygiene discipline;
- student portfolio creation;
- fee, pricing, and small-business readiness;
- measurable outcomes after the first batch.

## 3. Public-Source Market Context

### 3.1 Sector validation

Beauty and wellness training is a recognized vocational-skilling category in India. The Beauty & Wellness Sector Skill Council (B&WSSC), promoted by CII with support from NSDC and operating under the skill-development ecosystem, lists beauty and salon sub-sectors such as skincare, hair dressing, makeup, cosmetology, and nail care. B&WSSC also reports large-scale enrollment, assessment, and certification activity across the sector.

Relevant source: https://www.bwssc.in/

The NSDC National Occupational Standards listing states that Beauty & Wellness Sector Skill Council standards are NSQF-aligned/NSQC-cleared and can be used by training organizations to build curriculum, subject to updates from the Sector Skill Council.

Relevant source: https://nsdcindia.org/nos-listing/5

### 3.2 Kerala and Thiruvananthapuram context

Thiruvananthapuram is a suitable market for a premium practical institute because the city has high literacy, strong service-sector exposure, and a dense urban catchment. The Government of Kerala's Thiruvananthapuram district demography page reports Thiruvananthapuram city literacy at 95.10%, with female literacy at 93.78% as per Census 2011.

Relevant source: https://trivandrum.gov.in/demography/

Kerala also has an established women self-employment ecosystem through Kudumbashree. A Local Self Government Department report states that Kudumbashree supports women entering self-employment through orientation training, entrepreneurship development training, skill development training, and financial supports.

Relevant source: https://lsgkerala.gov.in/en/kudumbashree/news/kudumbashree-provides-employment-323-lakh-women-through-163458-micro-enterprise

## 4. Customer Segments

### 4.1 Job seekers

These students want salon employment, basic confidence, service discipline, and certificate credibility. They are likely to compare Soorya's Institute against branded academies, government-linked programs, and local parlour classes.

What they need:

- skin, hair, makeup, and hygiene basics;
- salon etiquette and client communication;
- practical practice time;
- interview preparation and portfolio photos;
- placement or referral support where possible.

### 4.2 Freelance and home-based beauticians

These students want to earn from home or on-call services. They need a service menu, pricing guidance, WhatsApp/Instagram promotion, customer handling, and safe product use.

What they need:

- small starter service menu;
- home-service hygiene standards;
- pricing templates;
- before/after portfolio process;
- festival, bridal, and local-event marketing.

### 4.3 Future small-parlour owners

These students want to start a small ladies' parlour or upgrade an informal service activity into a business.

What they need:

- core beauty services;
- product and consumable costing;
- appointment handling;
- basic accounting;
- customer retention;
- licensing, GST threshold awareness, and local compliance guidance from a qualified professional when needed.

## 11. Compliance and Risk Notes

### 11.1 GST and tax

GST treatment should be confirmed with a local CA because training, beauty services, and product/kit supply may have different tax implications depending on registration status, turnover, invoice structure, and service classification. Public tax portals commonly describe GST registration thresholds and beauty/wellness service taxation, but final treatment should come from a qualified professional.

Action: consult a CA before collecting course fees.

### 11.2 Certification

Soorya's Institute can issue its own participation/completion certificate from the beginning. However, the certificate should not imply government recognition unless an actual affiliation/approval exists.

Action: explore B&WSSC/NSDC-aligned curriculum mapping and future affiliation, but do not delay the pilot batch waiting for it.

### 11.3 Quality risk

The biggest operational risk is trying to teach 15 students practically at once in a space originally assessed for 5-10 students.

Control: split practical sessions into two groups, maintain attendance records, use practical checklists, and cap active practical stations.

### 11.4 Tutor dependency

Soorya is the key trainer. This is a strength for trust, but also a dependency.

Control: document lesson plans, practical checklists, student materials, and assessment rubrics. Bring guest trainers for selected one-day modules if needed.

### 11.5 Admissions risk

At INR 60,000, students will expect a serious program, kit clarity, practical training, and visible career value.

Control: publish a clear syllabus, payment plan, kit inclusion/exclusion, attendance rules, refund rules, and expected outcomes before collecting deposits.

## 12. Go/No-Go Criteria

Proceed with the first batch if these conditions are met:

- at least 12 students pay the admission booking amount;
- practical training timetable is split into two groups;
- setup purchases are limited to the first-batch need;
- Malayalam brochure and syllabus PDF are ready before marketing;
- CA confirms fee invoicing and GST/tax approach;
- student kit and consumables policy is finalized;
- app or simple tracker is ready for enquiries, fees, and attendance.

Do not proceed with the full 15-student model if fewer than 10 students enroll. In that case, either postpone by 2-4 weeks or run a smaller pilot with adjusted costs.

## 13. Final Recommendation

Soorya's Institute should proceed with a controlled first batch of 15 students at INR 60,000 for 4 months, provided the batch is split for practical training and the launch is marketed as a premium, founder-led salon apprenticeship program.

The business is feasible because it combines:

- an existing parlour premises and brand identity;
- a proprietor-trainer with direct expertise;
- strong local relevance for women seeking income skills;
- a fee level that is supported by comparable professional-course benchmarks;
- additional rental income from external trainer sessions;

The most important success factor is not the classroom setup. It is admission conversion and student outcome quality. If the first batch produces visible student work, testimonials, and confident graduates, the second and third batches can be marketed at equal or higher pricing with stronger credibility.

## 15. Investor Diligence Additions

Before committing the full launch budget, an intelligent investor should ask for the following additions:

### 15.1 Primary demand validation

Public sources are useful, but they are not enough. Run a 30-day admissions validation campaign before large purchases.

Track:

- number of enquiries by source;
- counselling conversations completed;
- demo-day attendance;
- objections heard most often;
- number of students willing to pay INR 10,000 booking amount;
- conversion rate from enquiry to booking.

Decision rule: proceed confidently only if at least 12 students pay the booking amount.

### 15.2 Competitor mystery-shopping

Call or visit 5-7 competing providers in Thiruvananthapuram and nearby areas.

Capture:

- course fee;
- course duration;
- kit inclusion;
- language of instruction;
- certificate type;
- practical hours;
- placement support claims;
- batch size;
- payment plan;
- refund policy.

This will sharpen Soorya's pricing, syllabus, and counselling pitch.

### 15.3 Unit economics per student

The study should track variable cost per student separately from fixed cost.

Minimum fields:

- kit cost;
- consumables cost;
- certificate/material cost;
- payment collection cost;
- expected bad debt or delayed payment risk.

This prevents the INR 60,000 fee from looking profitable while hidden student-level costs grow.

### 15.4 Capacity and timetable proof

Because the space is comfortable for 5-10 students at one time, the 15-student batch must be supported by a clear timetable.

Required proof:

- Group A and Group B practical calendar;
- station-wise capacity;
- trainer hours per week;
- parlour-client conflict plan;
- hygiene reset time between sessions.

### 15.5 Outcome and placement proof

For batch one, define success before training starts.

Suggested outcome metrics:

- 90% course completion;
- 80% students complete a practical portfolio;
- at least 10 students able to perform core services independently under supervision;
- at least 5 students earn, intern, freelance, or interview within 90 days of completion;
- collect at least 8 usable testimonials.

### 15.6 Legal, tax, and certificate clarity

Before admissions:

- CA to confirm GST and invoicing treatment;
- written refund and attendance policy;
- certificate wording reviewed so it does not imply government recognition unless formally obtained;
- photo/video consent form for student portfolios and marketing.

### 15.7 Downside plan

If only 8-10 students enroll, do not cancel automatically. Run a smaller pilot only if setup spend is reduced and the timetable is simplified.

Decision options:

- 12-15 paid bookings: launch full batch;
- 8-11 paid bookings: launch lean pilot or extend admissions by 2-4 weeks;
- fewer than 8 paid bookings: postpone and revise offer, pricing, or marketing.
